Meet Our Board of Directors!

We're Looking For Board Members!

We're looking for new Board Members! If you have nonprofit experience and want to assist the LGBTQ+ community in and around Berks County, consider filling out the application below!

​

Submit the application to Laura Biancone at lbiancone@lgbtcenterofreading.com!

Download the Application Here!

Kai Fawn Miller (she/her) - Vice President

As a proud ally of the LGBT community, Kai has served on the board of directors since 2020 and brings her extensive experience in leadership, communication, and outreach to the LGBT Center. She is the Chief Experience Officer of Emerald One, a leadership consulting firm, the owner of Graystone Farm, a local welcoming wedding venue focused on providing couples with the intimate experience they want on the budget they have, and the head coach of the Schuylkill Valley Junior High Girls soccer team. Kai lives on a 270-year-old farm in Ontelaunee Township with her partner, David Diaz, and her two amazing daughters. 

Charles Corbit III (he/him) - Treasurer

Charles Corbit is an outspoken and staunch ally of the LGBTQ+ community. Charles has over 25 years of experience in senior management positions with global responsibilities. He is currently a partner at ERM, a global sustainability consultancy, where he provides strategic and advisory support for Fortune 100 companies on their environmental, social, and transformation journeys. 

 

Over 18 years ago, a personal turning point occurred for Charles when he became aware that some of his family and friends did not have the same rights as he did due to who they loved. Since then, he spends a significant amount of his time working for equality and economic justice. Charles lives with his wife, Bernice Hines Corbit, in Exeter Township, who share the same passion for social and political activism. Oh, and they have four really cool cats.

Jen Banasavage (she/her)

Jennifer Basanavage has been a long time advocate for the LGBTQ+ community. Jennifer is a chef and restaurateur at Bella Trattoria in Spring City PA. She provides a welcoming space to work for community youth as well as a welcoming space for community guests.

 

With over 20 years of hospitality experience, Jennifer brings her expertise in party and event planning to the center. She is excited to head the events committee in bringing fantastic events to support and raise much needed funds for the center. With so much gravity in the world, we need to continue to celebrate who we are and what we do.

 

Jennifer lives in Pottstown with longtime partner Philip, daughter Zofia, pups Wolfgang, Aloyisious, Josie, and countless foster dogs. 

Claire Cummings (she/her)

For more than 17 years, Claire has worked in operations leadership, where she loves turning challenges into opportunities for growth. She believes in building strong systems that support people, and in leading with both empathy and clear direction. In her current role as Vice President of Operations for Photogenic Inc., Claire has guided the company through national growth while keeping teams connected and supported.

She looks forward to using her experience to help the Center run smoothly, grow thoughtfully, and make an even greater difference in the community.

Isaac Gonzalez-Perez (he/him)

Isaac Gonzalez-Perez is a veteran of The United States Navy, Owner and Executive Director of The Greater Reading Academy for Culinary Education, Executive Chef and Social Entrepreneur from the Greater Reading area. 

 

With over 25 years of continued service in the Restaurant and Hospitality industry, Chef Isaac has lead kitchen teams in top performing restaurants in Philadelphia, The Greater Baltimore area and right here in his hometown of Reading. 

 

Now, as the Founder of The Greater Reading Academy for Culinary Education, he strives for the betterment of humanity through acts of service, a commitment to excellence and by fostering a culture of growth and responsibility to Community.

 

Isaac also serves on the Board of Directors for The Forge of Reading, a local 501 (c)(3) that works towards the betterment of men in the community through positive imagery, fellowship and acts of service.

Johnathan Rodriguez-Baez (he/him)

Johnathan Rodríguez-Báez is the Manager of Bilingual Education & Programs Access at Planned Parenthood Keystone, with over five years of experience advancing inclusive and affirming sexual and reproductive health education. Based in Reading, PA, he brings extensive experience working with LGBTQ+ communities through programming focused on safer sex, consent, healthy relationships, and equitable access to care.

 

Johnathan holds a BA in Education from the InterAmerican University of Puerto Rico and is currently pursuing a master’s degree in Clinical Social Work at Widener University, with additional graduate-level coursework in Human Sexuality. His professional and volunteer experiences reflect a strong commitment to equity, access, and community-centered leadership.

 

Outside of his professional work, Johnathan lives in Reading with his husband and their two cats, enjoys movies and series, and values thoughtful conversations that foster connection and understanding.

Melissa Medina (she/her)

Melissa Medina is a longtime LGBTQ+ ally with over ten years of nonprofit experience, including serving as Congregational Administrator of the Unitarian Universalist Church. She lives in Leesport, Pennsylvania with her husband and two children and is the parent of a transgender child, which has given her firsthand insight into the importance of affirmation and resources for LGBTQ+ youth and families. Melissa is honored to bring her experience, perspective, and commitment to the LGBTQ+ Center’s Board of Directors.
